The Kariparamba Sahityotsav conducted by SSF (Sunni Students Federation) is an esteemed literary event that has been running for 31 years. This indicates a longstanding tradition of celebrating literature and fostering literary discussions within the community. With each passing year, such festivals evolve, attracting more participants, exploring diverse themes, and contributing to the enrichment of literary culture.


Cerebrating Values

In Vaikom Muhammed Basheer's novel Ormayude Arakal, the scene where Basheer, who returns home after a long time, is invited to eat by his mother is presented in a very touching way. Basheer, who has traveled around the world for many years and finally reached his own home, is welcomed by his mother as if it were an ordinary day. There are no dramas or celebrations. The fact that his son has traveled all over the world is not an issue for his mother. For his mother, Basheer will always be her only son. Basheer describes this scene by saying that he has understood one of the greatest truths in his life. No matter how much time passes, we should try not to let a rift in relationships occur, and the SF Kariparamba Unit Literary Festival points to the values ​​of relationships that are becoming alienated.
